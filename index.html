Resat Amin Priya

Hi there! I am a peer educator at Katha and my focus is on combating the culture of gender-based violence in Bangladesh through sexuality education, research, and interactive dialogue. I have completed my bachelor's degree with a major in Law and minor in Anthropology from BRAC University. I became interested in the intersection of law and digital anthropology during my undergraduate studies. I am passionate about digital anthropology, social justice and social rights issues in developing countries.

I was born and raised in Dhaka, Bangladesh, a city of over 16 million people. I have always enjoyed playing instruments and cooking, and love learning new things. I am always up for conversations about books-to-read, society, feminism and politics. I enjoy meeting new people and exploring new coffeeshops in Dhaka.

Sometimes I sound really a depressive one but deep inside I am so optimistic about life and the earth.

I am currently for looking for research opportunities (also graduate position) in the intersection of law, digital humanities and gender! I have experience in conducting field studies, interviewing, socio-legal analysis, qualitive research and report writing.
